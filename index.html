About Me

Urban researcher and data analyst bridging spatial methods with equitable policy outcomes.

I am a candidate for an MS in Urban Informatics at Northeastern University (GPA 3.72, expected April 2026), with a BA in Social Sciences (Economics & Geography) from the University of Cape Coast, Ghana.

My work sits at the intersection of computational methods and community-centered inquiry. As a Data Analytics Intern with the Committee to Repeal the Newton Overnight Parking Ban (2025), I analyzed parking violation records, excise tax data, and Census data to support a municipal ballot initiative. I also collaborated with the Metropolitan Area Planning Council (MAPC) on heat protection policy research for Massachusetts renters.

Earlier in my career I served as a Data Registration Officer and Field Data Officer in Ghana, where I collected biometric data for national ID systems and used GIS mapping software to track city road infrastructure. I am particularly interested in how data methods can surface inequities that traditional planning tools miss.
