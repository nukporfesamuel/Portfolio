Housing Preservation vs. Redevelopment in Boston

Parcel-Level GIS Multivariate Regression Community-Based Research Housing Policy

Overview

When investment flows into Boston neighborhoods, does it strengthen homes that residents already rely on — or replace them? This question was raised by community partners at the Dudley Street Neighborhood Initiative (DSNI) during participatory workshops in Roxbury.

To answer it, I constructed a custom Preservation Ratio (renovation investment ÷ total investment) from 691,985 building permits across 2,146 census blocks (2006–2024). Multivariate regression revealed that demographics do not predict preservation — but corporate ownership does, with a striking spatial reversal on Blue Hill Avenue likely driven by Community Development Corporations.
