Project 02 · GIS for Urban & Regional Policy · Northeastern University

Equitable Access to Green Space in Boston

Environmental Justice Census Data GIS / Spatial Join Choropleth Mapping

Overview

Roxbury and Dorchester rank first and second in Boston for raw park count — yet their residents face significant green space inequities. This project exposed that paradox by shifting from raw park counts to a parks-per-1,000-residents metric using 2020 Census data, spatially joined to neighborhood boundaries in ArcGIS.

The two choropleth maps — one showing raw counts, one showing per-capita access — tell opposite stories about the same city, revealing that standard metrics systematically misidentify which communities need investment. Downtown, Leather District, and Harbor Islands have effectively zero parks per capita yet are invisible in raw count analyses.
